BANYAN TREE

      


SCIENTIFIC NAME : Ficus benghalensis

COMMON NAME : Banyan Tree

ORIGIN : India

SCIENTIFIC CLASSIFICATION:
  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Angiosperms
  • Order: Rosales
  • Family: Moraceae
  • Genus: Ficus
CULTIVATION TIPS:
Sunlight :
1. Banyan trees thrive in full sunlight.
2. They should receive at least 6-8 hours of direct sunlight each day.
3. They can tolerate partial shade, but full sunlight promotes optimal growth.
Watering :
1. Young banyan trees require regular watering to establish roots.
2. Mature trees are drought-tolerant but benefit from occasional deep watering.
3. Ensure the soil is well-draining to prevent waterlogging, which can harm the roots.

Soil Type:
1. Banyan trees prefer rich, well-draining soil.
2. They thrive in a variety of soil types but prefer slightly acidic to neutral pH (6.0-7.5).
3. Soil should be enriched with organic matter, such as compost, to support growth.
4. Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot, especially in young trees.

USES:
  • Shade: Provides extensive shade due to its large canopy, making it ideal for parks and large gardens.
  • Medicinal: Used in traditional medicine for its various health benefits, such as treating infections and inflammation.
  • Cultural: Holds significant cultural and religious importance in many regions, often planted near temples and public places.
  • Ecological: Supports various wildlife, including birds and small mammals, by providing habitat and food.
  • Ornamental: Used as an impressive focal point in large landscapes and botanical gardens.
