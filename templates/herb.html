MINT

      


SCIENTIFIC NAME : Mentha

COMMON NAME : Mint

ORIGIN : United States

SCIENTIFIC CLASSIFICATION:
  • Kingdom: Plantae
  • Division: Angiosperms
  • Order: Lamiales
  • Family: Lamiaceae
  • Genus: Mentha
CULTIVATION TIPS :-
Sunlight :
1. Mint thrives in partial to full sunlight.
2. Mint should receive about 4-6 hours of sunlight each day.
3. In hotter climates, some afternoon shade can be beneficial to prevent the plant from drying out.
Watering :
1. Mint prefers consistently moist soil but should not be waterlogged.
2. Water the plant regularly, ensuring the soil stays evenly moist.
3. Avoid letting the soil dry out completely between waterings.

2. Mint can tolerate a range of temperatures but may struggle in extreme heat or cold.
3. In cooler climates, mint can survive mild frosts, but protection is needed in severe cold.
Soil Type:
1. Mint prefers rich, well-draining soil.
2. A slightly acidic to neutral pH range of 6.0 to 7.0 is ideal.
3. Soil should be enriched with organic matter, such as compost, to enhance fertility and moisture retention.
4. Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot, as mint does not like to sit in soggy soil.

USES:
  • Flavoring: Adds a fresh taste to foods and drinks like salads, desserts, and teas.
  • Digestive Aid: Helps with digestion and relieves indigestion.
  • Aromatherapy: Provides a calming scent and refreshes indoor air.
  • Insect Repellent: Naturally repels insects like mosquitoes and ants.
  • Skincare: Used in products for its soothing and cooling effects on the skin
